Data & Methods

How this works

Every map is built from the same source material the Secretary of State uses to certify results.

Election data is derived from official Cast Vote Records (CVRs) published by the Maine Secretary of State. CVRs contain ballot-level ranked-choice data — each row is one ballot, with each voter's ranked choices recorded in order. This is distinct from summary totals: it allows round-by-round simulation at the town level.

Per-town vote totals are computed by grouping CVR ballots by precinct and simulating RCV elimination rounds ballot-by-ballot. Where precincts include ward or district suffixes (e.g. "Bangor W1"), ballots are merged into the parent municipality before aggregation.

District-level totals are validated against official certified results from the Maine Secretary of State. Where CVR sample sizes produce minor rounding differences from official totals, per-candidate scale factors are applied to align round-one totals exactly to certification. Intermediate rounds are estimated from sample ballot proportions.

UOCAVA (Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act) ballots are included in district totals but are not assigned to a specific municipality and do not appear on the town map. They are tracked separately in the data.

Source files: Maine Secretary of State — Election Results & Reporting

About

Maine RCV Map

What this is

A public-interest project tracking Maine ranked-choice voting results at the town level. Built to make the mechanics of RCV visible — not just who won, but how the count resolved across geography.

How to use the maps

Step through rounds using the stepper or arrow keys. Hover any town for a breakdown of votes and transfers that round. The sidebar tracks district-level totals as the count progresses.

Data source

Official cast vote records from the Maine Secretary of State. District totals validated against certified SOS results. Code and data are available on GitHub.
